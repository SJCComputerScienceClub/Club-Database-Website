Mission Statement

InLight is a student-led platform created to spark conversations, breathe nuance into the biased, and uplift voices that would otherwise go unheard. In the spirit of St. John Baptist De La Salle, InLight aims to serve our community by giving everyone a voice, no matter their background. We hope to unite our diverse DMV community through our common home: St. Johns.

Letter from the Editor

As protests and social uprisings have flooded the streets of Iran regarding the death of Mahsa Amini, women have fought back against years of oppression and discrimination. Not only does the unfair treatment of women affect those living in Iran, but also in many other countries to this day. I personally resonated with this news partly because of my Persian background, but also because of the lack of representation women still have today in the United States. Obtaining freedom is when an individual has the power to speak their mind, write as they please, and act in the manner they choose. I wished to portray this theme in creative ways with our wonderful contributors for this years publication. I want to thank Ms. Turowski, our librarian, for offering up the library for our meetings and Ms. Gelso, our moderator, for supporting us throughout the year and giving an amazing group of students the platform to create the work that they wanted. We are capable of using our unique voices to create change and fight against unequal powers. We have the strength to speak out together and support each other, even across seas. Please encourage others to use their voices in great ways that benefit us all and continue to fight for what you believe in, it matters. I hope you all enjoy the St. Johns 2023 InLight publication, I know I do!

Leila Sarkarzadeh 23, Editor in Chief

Face to Face

Freedom from the mind, body, and soul. In order to progress we have to learn to step outside of ourselves and see the world from a different perspective. Through breaking free of limitations, we can achieve freedom from ourselves. We have to let go of our insecurities and our fears and embrace our strengths. For some, this artwork may depict two different sides of the same face, but for others the piece is of two faces becoming one. Freedom has a different meaning depending on perspective as well. Freedom could mean political liberty, the right to vote and speak freely, or it could signify the ability to pursue ones dreams and goals, or the ability to choose ones own path, or even the freedom from a negative state of mind.

Snatched

Kellie Gilliam 26

To write a piece on freedom well, that could be controversial. If there is one particular idea whose use and definition were never agreed upon, freedom is it. But, for this assignment, Ill write my peace.

Most people want to do the right thing, I hope. But I think people do. To be left alone, to not be confronted by wrong. To not be told that decency doesnt fit. I think thats the real definition [of freedom]: having the ability to do the right thing. Doing whats ethical for the sake of it just because it is right.

Right. Correct? Truth? All of these? Right is all the good values the Lord gave to us. It is His call to be better, to be nicer. It is the opposite of bullying, the opposite of lying, and the twin of respect. And respect doesnt have to be the same as agreement ever.

So, this concluding line right, and the ability to do it, is a common longing. A freedom no person should snatch from another. A godly freedom this world needs.

(Un)Natural Virtues

Phoebe Sood 25

Nature forgives but I dont.

Sometimes I get consumed by a deep anger, a hatred unwilling to greet forgiveness;

Swept up in a windstorm of irritation that soon transports me to a place of regret.

Nature forgives,

Nature shares,

Why cant I?

The dead leaf finally stops hanging on to the tree,

The tide erases imprints in the sands,

Nature lets go.

Maybe I should too.

Rivers and streams flow,

But I am a dam; I disrupt the flow and I dont let forgiveness wash away my emotions.

I leech on to my anger,

And instead of letting it wash away from me I choose to erode.

Nature finds freedom in forgiving;

Maybe someday I will too.

I often grapple with the thought of freedom. Are we are all truly free or rather, trapped in a capitalist society driven by money? What does it mean to be free in a modern world of infinite possibilities? In this piece, I portray the confusion and confliction I experience when trying to rationalize the idea of freedom. Furthermore, it is an expression of concern and skepticism, in regard to American and civil freedom. I included an image of a person experiencing homelessness in America, yet also the Statue of Liberty. I painted a free floating figure falling into an infinite abyss, yet in the opposing corner, I drew a cage, trapping a flying bird. I added parts of Amanda Gormans inauguration speech throughout, of which she speaks of our countrys struggle to find freedom and of a new, promising dawn. This piece is meant to provoke viewers, leaving them to also ponder, question, and seek ultimate freedom.

- Kalei Serra 23

The Black American dream

Karis Francis 23

Do yall remember what happened to Rosa Parks? The bus situation. The sudden inclination to stay sittin in that seat. To say, Mister, I aint goin to move my feet. The bravery, the courage, the strength. The fear, the sadness, the pain. All bottled up inside her, ready to explode in a burst of red flames. The Black American Dream. To survive and to be seen. To thrive and be free. To know that we mean something. Something more than just our past. To know we live in a nice world. A world where were safe. To know we can take the bus without an officer yelling. Get up! Where we can say no, without a violent show, without the blood stains and the vivid, hurtful sayings. Without the n-word with the hard er, without being removed from our own cars. Where its peaceful and calm. Where the mood is gentle and were as strong as the walls.

My grandma. I look back in his-story. But, I wanna tell her-story. 1938 is the year. My grandma was struck with fear. Just 13 years old. Doin what her momma told. Got on a bus. Sat in a seat in the middle. Books in hand. Told not to stand. Momma said, Stay in that seat until you see your sister. Didnt know she had to deal with Mister. Goin from Raleigh to Concord. A stop in Greensboro.

She got on the bus. Not expecting a fuss. They stopped. An old white man got on. The bus driver came to her and said, Move to the back. Hanging tightly to her sack, she gave a confident No. He said, Get up, girl. You got to go! She said again, No. As she sat there, with a deep stare, the man with a glare and on-edge hair, growled, Move or Ill call the law! Hanging on the last straw, she said, No. My momma told me not to move. So with a pep in his step, the man lumbered off the bus to get the police. She sat there, her butt glued to the seat, as the man rushed on following the police. Get up or Ill arrest you! the policeman exclaimed. As she got up, her mood completely changed. Looking around, she figured everyone was scared, or just didnt really care. She cried in silence, sitting alone. Longing for her mother, no longer strong as a stone.

The Black American Dream. To survive and be free. For all Black Americans. Even the people who ask for money in cans. Its fair and acceptable. It doesnt have to be rare and it can be obtainable. Some people have the same chance. Whether or not they take it is where they stand. The choice is to survive and be free, and strive to be strong as a tall tree. Or whimper and cry and live your life in a lie. Again, its just the Black American Dream.

I took this photograph in Acadia, Maine, after being amazed by the raw and grandiose beauty of our natural world. The infinitism of nature reminds me of our inherent natural freedomour freedom to explore the world and all its wonders, which lies at the tip of our fingers. The world and nature itself, is free for us to venture into and discover. Only when it is corrupted by our own selves, does it no longer become free. This image symbolizes this natural and organic kind of freedom, one that is pure and innocent.

When were young we internalize only what were told

of what our mothers and fathers think and some of what they feel

of what their parents told them about the world and what they believe is real

We are but shadow; a perfect portrait of face

Freedom of imperfection

The freedom of imperfection is one that took me years to learn. When first attempting to master the art of photography, I thought about the formulas and rules that would result in the perfect image. I used a camera the size of me, spending hours attempting to capture what I thought would be interesting and beautiful to others. In my attempt to be perfect, I was so far from free.

This picture represents freedom because it is so far from perfect. My two favorite things, sailboats and the moon, are captured in a skewed and blurred sense. I found freedom when I stopped caring about the rules made by others. I learned that I am allowed to form my own definition of beauty, I am not tied to the standards put in place by others. This picture represents freedom because I feel free to appreciate my own beautiful images.

The Crow

Sadie Idler 23

We would run through the hills, bare feet turning pale in the cold grassy dew, green grass spread out around us, chalky cliffs before us and blue sky above. We ran, jumping, laughing, free. She held her arms out like a bird and ran to the tallest hill, twirling in the crisp spring air, staring at the sky. We collapsed, dresses wet with dew, hair wild with mountain air. Our backs damp on the grass and our fingers pointing at the sky, she would say lets fly away, like crows, that way the wild can be our home and we can be wild.

I never realized how much she had to fly away from, her raven hair frolicking in the wind the way we did in the field, curls dancing in the air the way we danced in the grass. It was the only part of her that was free.

One day, I ran to the hills, looking for her curls, the footprints of crushed grass leading the way to her wild, flying heart. But I found none. Like so many others, she was a memory, a wild thing to be scorned then forgotten. Did they not understand that wild meant free?

Spring turned to fall, the grass faded, the sky turned grey, the leaves fell, crunching under my feet like the quiet whispers of a friend. Soon white coated the world, but missing from it was her black curls highlighting the beauty of snow. Round and round the sun went, green to brown to white to green. Then. Black. To the hills I walk, and one day, I see black, a color I have not seen since the free days of childhood, when freedom was a given and not a rarity. I drop my basket, chores forgotten, shoes placed carefully in the grass, for I know I will have to retrieve them later, like a brushed aside responsibility one cannot avoid. I run, my pale feet leaving crushed grass behind me, blue sky above, my fiery red curls painting the sky red. The crow sits, head cocked, at the top of the hill, eyes round and thoughtful. Im wild now, the crow seems to say. I fly the way my soul did, the ground no longer claims me. I stop, breath fogging the air, cheeks pink from the chill, feet and dress damp from dew. The crow cocks its wild head, feathers rustling in the wind, then away, catching the breeze that whispers to me, flee, fly. I fly up the hill, feet pounding the earth I am bound to, arms spread wide. I want to catch the air the way she did. But away she flies, fading into the clouds, the blue sky enveloping her wild soul. I run below her, a shadow on the grass knowing Ill never catch up. Breath fogging up the air, I stop, cheeks red and legs aching, I stand, arms spread wide, surrounded by green earth, running, flying, alive, but without. But alive. It took me so long. But I am alive again.

Embrace and release

Leire Sofia Rossi-Crespo 23

Part I

Wake Up!

I lay, embraced, with a gift gushing through my warm, young blood, knocking on my strong, beating heart and echoing to the hairs that stood,

rigid,

on my arms like antennas.

I could feel it;

growing more and more as new shapes and colors flooded my eyes, as new smells flew up my nose,

and as new sounds and voices tickled my eardrum.

I could not escape it,

it buzzed,

and itched

and begged.

But soon,

what seemed to beget uncertainty,

became the key

the key to twinkling creativity and rough-edged courage and unbounded potential.

Years later, I came to know this gift was what-you-call curiosity.

Youre the Catalyst

I lie; this free-floating gift wandering about me.

Was I supposed to store it somewhere forever?

Was I supposed to cut it open and see what was inside?

Was I supposed to embrace it?

What if I got some new wrapping paper and a ribbon?

What color?

What size?

What design?

Wasnt one size supposed to fit all?

No, I recalled, it was up to me.

The curiosity was mine, it would always be mine,

it would yell and smile,

in spunk and style,

and even then,

squeeze and stretch my mind.

Mind to

Body and Body to

Mind,

I was free.

The words, the colors, the smells, the voices drove around my mind,

no seatbelt,

no airbag,

just me.

Knowledge is boundless,

Potential is boundless,

Curiosity is boundless,

does that make me boundless?

Yes, I recalled, but it was up to me.

What if others took it from me?

What if others disapproved of me?

What if, I, believed in me?

To embrace is to believe,

and to believe is to love,

and if all I ought to do was clench my hands and grip with my fingers,

could I, alone, love

me?

Yes, yes, yes, my mind screeched,

be free!

Part II

Intellect is Power

The mind is boundless;

power is within me.

If only I used it,

I could only agree.

I cannot guarantee,

but certainly not disagree,

as with knowledge and wit

you only have possibility.

To feel,

and will,

and reason is

to be. And to be offers choice,

and with choice one can be free.

Curiosity sparks and kicks, and

soon,

equips the mind to fix.

To fix is to reason,

and to reason is to reflect,

and to reflect is to grow. And so, as I

and my mind,

grow, extend and aspire

I become responsible for me,

my potential,

until I am free.

Power is Freedom

Ones potential,

curiosity,

intellect,

emotions are within;

they scratch and

burn and

kick, but are the very fibers of our woven being;

the threads of which may itch and wear,

but that will always bear

the shakes of despair.

Strong are those shakes and kicks,

strong are those threads,

strong is your mind,

all of which should not enkindle dread.

Dread is energy,

just in disguise,

that with a little make-up it may not be all you surmise;

instead, it may surprise your closed eyes

that one day you may devise

the perfect plan to rise.

Not to brag, inflate or drag,

but simply to tag

the great success of the strings and ropes that hang within me.

Ones strength and power zeal to fulfill

ones every skill: the checks in the boxes that kill.

Be the shield and be your light as

if you gather and embrace your might,

the plight that once pulled and teared

will no longer be feared;

the path is clear,

you are your own pioneer,

breathe and grasp,

your success is near.

Choose your path and embrace the rocks,

as soon you will find that will open your locks.

Part III

Open Your Arms

Open your mind,

open your heart,

open your arms,

and look at the stars.

You are the one,

just believe

that by opening your mind

you will find the freedom to remind

you and others

of your shine.

Shine,

shine,

shine,

and shine again

because with persistence

there is little resistance.

Reach,

reach,

reach,

and reach again

because with an open-mind

comes the power of an ocean tide.

Anything can be Something

An egg and flour can go into a cake,

or a cookie

or a tart

or a macaroon.

And just like an egg and some flour,

we have options

worth adoption that will make us blossom and free.

But what would happen if we didnt have an egg?

There would be no cake,

or cookie,

or tart,

or macaroon.

Why only have scrambled eggs?

Embrace you,

embrace all,

and with that we will recall the beauty of our haul.

The beauty of freedom,

the beauty of unity,

the beauty in knowing we will have opportunity; to laugh and fight,

and cheer and scream

until the day

we are

all

let

free.
